It started with a desire to mod my favorite game—dicking around in XML files to change how characters looked. That rabbit hole taught me something valuable: code could reshape experiences. I was hooked.

Now I spend my days building tools that let AI models do what they do best—reason, analyze, decide—while actually doing things in the real world. MCP servers are my jam because they're the bridge between "that's interesting" and "that's actually useful."

By day, I work on internal tools that make other developers' lives easier. By night (or very early morning, let's be honest), I'm probably obsessing over DX improvements, writing documentation nobody asked for, or debugging something that will break in production.

If you've found yourself here, you're probably thinking about AI agents, MCP, or just want to see what someone's been building. Welcome. Pull up a chair.
